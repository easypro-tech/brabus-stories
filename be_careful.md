# Be Careful

Night in the hospital is always the same.

Dim light in the corridor.
Gleaming linoleum.
The occasional footsteps of the night nurse.

The child sleeps.

She sits beside him, watching him breathe.

Sometimes she leans closer.
Just to make sure.

---

Toward dawn, she decides to leave for an hour.

She needs to run home.
Change clothes.
Get back before he wakes.

She walks fast. Almost runs.

Afraid to be late.

Afraid he'll wake up and find himself alone.

In the corridor, she runs into a man.

A colleague from the Academy of Sciences.

He's used to seeing her at conferences.
Giving presentations.
With geological cross-section maps.

— What are you doing here?

— My child is in a room.

And she keeps walking.

Almost running.

---

The child grows up.

---

When her memory begins to fade, they meet her at the airport.

She's flying in from another country.

They arrive on time.

But the flight lands early.

When they enter the terminal, she's already gone.

They run through the terminal.

Nowhere.

Then he suddenly understands.

And runs to the taxi stand.

She's already sitting in a cab.

Calm.

The driver is about to pull away.

He opens the door and takes her hand.

She looks at him calmly.

Without alarm.

He leads her to the car.

A large dark SUV.

She gets in and studies the interior.

The dashboard.
The buttons.
The leather seats.

She looks carefully.

For a long time.

---

At a gas station, the daughter goes out to buy water.

Two of them remain in the car.

She looks around the interior again.

Then looks at him.

— Are you still doing the same thing?

He answers:

— Yes, Mom.

She is silent.

Then says quietly:

— Be careful.

---

He wasn't.

When she was dying,
he wasn't there.

---

The daughter was there.

Until the very last moment.

She sat beside her and heard her mother sometimes ask:

— Did he eat?

No name was spoken.

But an answer was always needed.

---

Memory fades in layers.

Faces.
Names.
Years.

And then, amid the emptiness, one thing remains.

— Did he eat?
